About the Institute

A tertiary cancer care, research and training centre under the Department of Health, Medical & Family Welfare, Government of Andhra Pradesh.

Overview

Tertiary cancer care for Andhra Pradesh

The State Cancer Institute, Kurnool is a 120-bedded exclusive tertiary cancer hospital under the Department of Health, Medical & Family Welfare, Government of Andhra Pradesh. It provides comprehensive oncology services across Medical, Surgical and Radiation Oncology, along with Onco-Pathology, Preventive Oncology and Palliative Care.

The Institute serves as a referral centre for the people of Andhra Pradesh and works closely with district hospitals, primary health centres and the AYUSH network for early detection, treatment and survivorship support.

  • Designated State Cancer Institute under the Strengthening of Tertiary Cancer Care scheme.
  • Affiliated with Kurnool Medical College for academic training.
  • Empanelled under Dr. YSR Aarogyasri and AB-PMJAY for cashless treatment.
  • ESMO-designated centre of integrated oncology and palliative care (one of 23 in India).
  • Participating in the ICMR-funded Hospital Based Cancer Registry (HBCR).

History

A brief history

The institute traces its origins to the cancer treatment unit attached to Kurnool Medical College. Over the years, the unit grew into a fully fledged department offering surgical, medical and radiation oncology.

Under the central scheme for Strengthening of Tertiary Cancer Care Facilities, the unit was upgraded to a State Cancer Institute, with expansion of infrastructure, recruitment of specialist faculty, induction of modern equipment including a linear accelerator and PET-CT, and accreditation initiatives that continue today.

The Institute is today a referral centre for cancer patients across Kurnool, Anantapur, Kadapa and Nandyal districts and beyond.
