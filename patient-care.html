Patient Care

Information for patients and families — OPD timings, admission process, financial assistance schemes and supportive services.

For First-time Visitors

Visiting the OPD

First-time visitors should report to the Registration Counter on the ground floor of the OPD block with a valid photo ID and any previous medical records or referral letters. Patients are then directed to the appropriate oncology OPD based on the suspected diagnosis or referral.

  • OPD timings: Mon – Sat, 9:00 AM – 1:00 PM.
  • Bring previous prescriptions, biopsy reports and imaging scans.
  • Aarogyasri / AB-PMJAY card if available — for cashless treatment.
  • Family member or attendant recommended for emotional support.

Financial Assistance

Schemes & support

Dr. YSR Aarogyasri

  • Cashless treatment for eligible cancer procedures.
  • Cards issued at the institute helpdesk.
  • Empanelled for surgical, medical & radiation oncology packages.

AB-PMJAY (Ayushman Bharat)

  • Up to ₹5 lakh annual cover per family.
  • Empanelled for cancer treatment packages.
  • Apply at the Pradhan Mantri Arogya Mitra desk.

Other Support

  • Chief Minister's Relief Fund — apply through District Collectorate.
  • Health Minister's Discretionary Grants.
  • NGO & charitable trust assistance — through the Medical Social Worker.

In-patient

Admission & discharge

Inpatient admissions are co-ordinated through the OPD consultation and the admissions office. Allocated bed details, expected duration of stay and pre-admission preparation are explained at the time of the OPD visit.

Discharge summaries are provided in English with a Telugu addendum where required. Follow-up appointments and prescription refills are listed clearly. A medical social worker is available for guidance on schemes, transport, and accommodation arrangements for patients travelling from other districts.

  • One attendant permitted per inpatient.
  • Visitor hours: 4:00 PM – 6:30 PM (general wards).
  • Food court & canteen on campus.
  • Free patient transport for follow-ups (selected catchment areas).

Emergency

Emergency & helpline

The Institute provides 24×7 oncology emergency cover. Patients with febrile neutropenia, uncontrolled bleeding, severe pain or chemotherapy-related emergencies should report directly to the Emergency Wing.

Helpline: +91 8518 000999 (24×7) · State CM Helpline: 1902
